Many artists have their own journeys in the local music scene, and their stories are personal and unique to themselves. Many established artists had gone through their own trials and troubles before making it to where they are today.

These are stories of various artists about their own experiences and insights over the course of their own journeys.

A STORY FROM

MOONWLK

I started as every musician in the universe would start. First with inspiration, then passion, and last up, until today, perseverance. Becoming successful, let alone noticed, in the industry or even the local "scene" does not happen with magic, nor does it happen overnight. Don't worry about pleasing people with trends or gimmicks. If they like what you like, then you will be happy.

The success all depends on two things: a humble attitude, and the connections that you make. The Philippines, although dense with talent, is very small in terms of area. Even if you don't play in giant shows, you'll never know which important figure will be watching you at that five person attended gig.

A STORY FROM

TURN ME ON HOLLY

Starting a band in the underground scene is an uphill battle. Especially when no one knows about your music. Looking for a place to play on and production houses to line you up for gigs is, of course, also difficult. But thankfully, there were Facebook groups looking for bands to line up bands in their bars. Although it wasn't the scene we wanted to be in, but it was a definitely a good start. We played a lot of "open for line up" gigs and since it was some kind of an open mic, we got to play with a lot of bands from different genres, from metal to reggae, you name it!

It took us one year of doing these type of shows before we started getting bigger gigs. We're still a long way to go, but the gigs are getting better, and social media and online streaming services are definitely helping us bridge the gap from our target audience and our music.

TASK #1

ESTABLISH YOUR IMAGE

It’s important to know what kind of artist or band you want to be going forward. The kind of sound you want to create and the aesthetic that guides the way your brand presents itself to the public are important things to consider.

TASK #2

PLAY YOUR FIRST GIG

Your first gig is always going to be important: it’s your first taste of playing on stage to a crowd of people. Whether that crowd of people range from being a handful to a stacked crowd, what matters is the experience you get from it.

TASK #3

RECORD A SINGLE

You have to get your sound out there, and one of the ways you can start that off is by releasing a song that you’ve written. You could record the song yourself, or you could find an independent record label and pay them to help you produce, mix, and master your song.

TASK #4

CREATE SOCIAL MEDIA PLATFORMS

Besides your sound, you have to make your presence in the local music scene known to everyone. Starting social media platforms is a good way to do that. You’ll give a chance for your audience to learn more about you and connect to you, and you can let them know your gig schedules and have an avenue for promoting yourself.

TASK #5

GIG REGULARLY

You have to keep playing: find different venues that you can perform at or ask your connections if you can play at their events. Playing regularly will help you to build up your reputation, to meet more people and make those connections in the local music scene.
Try playing at 3 different gigs before moving to the next task.

TASK #6

RECORD AN EP

An extended play! More than a single, but not quite an album. The process of creating your extended play is executed similarly to how you did your single, except there are more songs involved. Again, you could either do this on your own, or you could check out the database to see which record labels you could visit to help you out in producing your songs.

TASK #7

GET ON STREAMING PLATFORMS

Music streaming has become the more popular ways for an audience to access music and for artists to get their music out there to a bigger audience.

At this point, you should have completed your single and your EP. Try getting those songs on Spotify before continuing on to the next task.

TASK #8

RECORD AN ALBUM

Welcome to your last task! At this point, you should have gone through various gigs, recorded several songs, and made some friends and connections in the music scene.

Creating an album is a tough task: it’s going to include more songs and cost you a bit more if you want to produce audio with better quality.
